Baked Mac and Cheese

I bet you have not tried any baked mac and cheese recipes as delicious and cheesiest as this one. This mac and cheese is not for the lactose intollerant as it will contain lots of cheese, perfect for those instagram pictures!

Ingredients

  • 8 Ounces of Elbow Macaroni
  • 4 Tablespoons of Unsalted Butter
  • 1/4 Cups of All-Purpose Flour
  • 2 Cups of Milk
  • 1 Cup of Shredded Sharp Cheddar Cheese
  • 1 Cup of Mozzarella Cheese
  • 1/2 Teaspoons of Salt
  • 1/4 Teaspoons of Black Pepper
  • 1/4 Teaspoon of Paprika(optional)
  • 1/4 Cup of Grated Parmesan Cheese(optional, for topping)
  • Chopped Fresh Parsley, for Garnishing(optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the elbow macaroni according to the package Instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for about 1-2 minutes to make a roux.
  3. Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly to ensure no lumps form. Continue cooking and whisking until the mixture thickens, for about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Reduce heat to low and add the shredded cheddar cheese and mozzarella cheese to the saucepan. Stir until the cheese has melted and the sauce is smooth
  5. Season the cheese sause with salt, black pepper, and paprika(if using), and stir well to combine.
  6. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until the passta is evenly coated.
  7. Optional: preheat your oven to 350° F (175°C). Transfer the mac mac-and-cheese mixture to a greased baking dish. Sprinkle grated parmesan cheese on top for an extra cheesy crust
  8. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden and bubbly
  9. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired.
  10. Serve the mac and cheese while it's still hot and creamy. It makes a great comfort food dish on its own or can be paired with a side salad or steamed vegetables. Enjoy!
